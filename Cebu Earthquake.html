Cebu Earthquake Death Toll Rises to 69.

MANILA, Philippines At least 69 people have been confirmed dead after a powerful earthquake struck Cebu on Tuesday night. Authorities are racing against time in the critical first 24 hours to rescue survivors who may still be trapped under collapsed structures. The quake damaged several heritage churches homes, schools, and caused widespread power outages across the province.

According to Office of Civil Defense (OCD) Assistant Secretary Rafael Alejandro IV, most of the fatalities were reported in Bogo City with 30 deaths, followed by 22 in San Remigio, 10 in Medellin, five in Tabogon, and one each in Sogod and Tabuelan. He noted that the figures are still subject to validation.

Alejandro also reported that 147 people were injured, mostly in Bogo City with 119 cases. Other towns with injured residents include San Francisco (14), Tabuelan (5), Catmon (5), and Daanbantayan (4).

He explained that the high number of casualties was partly due to the timing of the quake, which struck at 9:59 p.m. when most people were asleep and unable to react quickly. The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (Phivolcs) recorded the magnitude 6.9 earthquake with an epicenter 21 kilometers northeast of Bogo City and a shallow depth of five kilometers.

Search And Rescue Efforts

Alejandro said rescuers are working within the golden hour the first 24 hours when chances of saving lives are highest. Many people are still believed to be trapped in debris. The OCD has yet to release official figures on missing persons, but rescue updates are expected soon.

Two military C-130 aircraft and coast guard ships are being deployed to support search and rescue teams. Neighboring countries like Singapore, Indonesia, and Malaysia have been alerted in case international aid is needed, though local responders are currently managing the operations.

The province of Cebu has been placed under a state of calamity, allowing the release of emergency funds and enforcing a 60-day price freeze on basic goods.

Lesson for "The Big One"

Alejandro emphasized that the incident should serve as a reminder to prepare for the feared Big One a magnitude 7.2 earthquake projected to hit Metro Manila. A 2019 study estimated such an event could cause up to 52,000 deaths and 500,000 injuries.

To strengthen preparedness, quarterly earthquake drills are being held in schools and government offices in the capital. The Philippines, located along the Pacific Ring of Fire, regularly experiences earthquakes and volcanic activity due to tectonic movements.
