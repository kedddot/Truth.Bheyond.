Paolo heads for China; LPA outside PAR likely to become tropical depression

After Severe Tropical Storm Paolo (Matmo), PAGASA is now monitoring a low pressure area that could become the next tropical cyclone to affect the Philippines.

MANILA, Philippines – All tropical cyclone wind signals were lifted by late Saturday morning, October 4, as Severe Tropical Storm Paolo (Matmo) continued to move farther away from the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR).

By 10 a.m. Saturday, Paolo was already about 440 kilometers west of Sinait, Ilocos Sur. The storm was heading west-northwest toward southern China at a speed of 20 kilometers per hour.

Paolo still packed maximum sustained winds of 110 km/h and gusts reaching up to 135 km/h. However, PAGASA said it might strengthen again into a typhoon over the West Philippine Sea later on Saturday.

At its strongest, Paolo was classified as a typhoon with maximum sustained winds of 130 km/h when it made landfall in Dinapigue, Isabela, at 9 a.m. on Friday, October 3. The storm brought moderate to heavy rain and strong winds—ranging from Signal No. 1 to Signal No. 4—mostly affecting Northern and Central Luzon.

Even as it moves farther away, Paolo’s trough or extension is still expected to bring scattered rain and thunderstorms to several areas including the Ilocos Region, Cordillera Administrative Region, Central Luzon, Metro Manila, Cavite, Batangas, and Occidental Mindoro on Saturday.

Its outer rainbands may also cause strong to gale-force winds over Batanes, northern mainland Cagayan including the Babuyan Islands, Apayao, Zambales, and Bataan throughout the day.

Meanwhile, sea conditions will remain moderate to rough in these areas:

  • Ilocos Region – waves reaching up to 3 meters
  • Batanes, Babuyan Islands, and Zambales – up to 2.5 meters
  • Mainland Cagayan, Isabela, and Bataan – up to 2 meters


Paolo is the 16th tropical cyclone to enter or form within the Philippines in 2025 and the first for October. PAGASA estimates that two to four tropical cyclones could develop or enter the country this month.

In addition, a new low pressure area (LPA) outside PAR has a high chance of intensifying into a tropical depression within 24 hours. As of 8 a.m. Saturday, the LPA was located about 2,300 kilometers east-northeast of extreme Northern Luzon.

According to PAGASA Weather Specialist Grace Castañeda, this LPA or possible tropical cyclone may enter PAR by Monday night, October 6, or Tuesday morning, October 7. Further weather updates are expected soon.
