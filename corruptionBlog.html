FLOOD CONTROL:

What billions look like: Visualizing the staggering scale of flood control corruption.

MANILA, Philippines—How big is one billion pesos? A lot of Filipinos haven’t even seen such money in their lifetime—yet the flood control scandal involved ₱1 billion pesos of money inside suitcases, presumably delivered to a government official. Even worse, ₱180 billion in participants that were linked to the scheme were frozen by the investigators.

Former engineers of the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) gave some of the most explosive statements that ₱1 billion of cash, not bank transfer, was directly given to Ako Bicol RRP. Elizaldy Co’s penthouse at the Shangri-La at the Fort in Taguig

The scale increased even further. An online report citding the Anti-Money Laundering Council (AMLC) found data that revealed that ₱180 billion had passed down through 427 bank accounts associated with four contractor firms owned by flood-control “king and queen” Curlee and Sarah Discaya—sums of money that surpass the suitcase deliveries linked to Co.

These figures represent more than mere statistics; figures aren’t just numbers on paper or footnotes in Senate hearings. They embody the staggering volumes of taxpayer money—supposedly pocketed through ghost projects, bid-rigging, and systemic corruption—that could have financed public services, filled classrooms, and supported millions of families in the Philippines.
